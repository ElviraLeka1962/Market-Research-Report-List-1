<p><p>The female emergency contraceptive pill market primarily consists of two types: Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel is a progestin-based option, effective when taken within 72 hours after unprotected intercourse, preventing ovulation. Ulipristal Acetate, a selective progesterone receptor modulator, offers a broader time frame, remaining effective up to 120 hours post-intercourse. Both types provide critical options for women seeking to prevent unintended pregnancies, with differing efficacy timelines and mechanisms, catering to varying consumer needs and preferences in reproductive health.</p></p>
