<p><p>Jet A, Jet A-1, and Jet B are types of aviation turbine fuels used in jet engines. Jet A is primarily used in the United States, with a higher freezing point (-40°C). Jet A-1, the most common global fuel, has a lower freezing point (-47°C) and added static dissipater. Jet B, a wide-cut fuel, is used in colder climates due to its lower freezing point (-60°C) and higher volatility, making it suitable for extreme conditions but less common due to handling risks.</p></p>
