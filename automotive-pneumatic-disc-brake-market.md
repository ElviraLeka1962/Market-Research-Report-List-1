<p><p>An automotive pneumatic disc brake is a braking system that uses compressed air to apply pressure on brake pads, which then clamp onto a rotor to slow or stop a vehicle. This system is commonly used in heavy-duty vehicles like trucks, buses, and trailers due to its reliability, durability, and ability to handle high loads. Pneumatic disc brakes offer advantages such as better heat dissipation, reduced brake fade, and improved performance under extreme conditions compared to traditional drum brakes.</p><p>The Automotive Pneumatic Disc Brake Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period.
