<p><p>The multimetal dielectric nanocomposites market is categorized into high-k and low-k types based on their dielectric constant properties. High-k multimetal dielectrics have a higher dielectric constant, enhancing capacitance and enabling miniaturization in electronic components, making them suitable for advanced semiconductor applications. In contrast, low-k multimetal dielectrics offer reduced dielectric constant, minimizing crosstalk and energy loss in integrated circuits, thus improving performance and efficiency. Both types play crucial roles in advancing modern electronic and optoelectronic devices.</p></p>
