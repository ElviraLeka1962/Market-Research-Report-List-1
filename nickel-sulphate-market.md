<p><p>Nickel Sulphate is an inorganic compound with the formula NiSO₄, commonly used in electroplating, battery production, and as a precursor for nickel-based catalysts. It is a key component in the manufacturing of lithium-ion batteries, particularly for electric vehicles (EVs), due to its role in producing nickel-rich cathode materials. The compound is available in both anhydrous and hydrated forms, with the hexahydrate (NiSO₄·6H₂O) being the most commercially significant.</p><p>The Nickel Sulphate Market is experiencing robust growth, driven by the rising demand for EVs and renewable energy storage systems. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.6% during the forecast period, fueled by advancements in battery technology and increasing investments in sustainable energy solutions. Additionally, the expansion of EV infrastructure and government policies promoting clean energy are further accelerating demand.</p><p>Geographically, Asia-Pacific dominates the market due to its strong EV manufacturing base and growing industrialization. However, North America and Europe are also witnessing significant growth, driven by stringent environmental regulations and increasing adoption of EVs. Challenges such as fluctuating nickel prices and supply chain disruptions may impact the market, but technological innovations and strategic partnerships are expected to mitigate these issues.</p></p>
