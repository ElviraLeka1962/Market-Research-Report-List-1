<p><p>Ethylene Dichloride (EDC) is primarily produced through two processes: Direct Chlorination and Oxychlorination. In Direct Chlorination, ethylene reacts with chlorine to produce EDC, offering high purity and efficiency. Oxychlorination involves reacting ethylene with hydrogen chloride and oxygen, enabling the recycling of by-products and reducing waste. Both methods cater to diverse industrial applications, including PVC production. Direct Chlorination is favored for its simplicity, while Oxychlorination is valued for its sustainability and cost-effectiveness in large-scale operations, shaping the EDC market dynamics.</p></p>
